# Splendor — Rules, Mechanics & Game Walkthrough

## 1. Overview

Splendor is a 2–4 player engine-building card game. Players act as Renaissance gem merchants, collecting gems to purchase development cards that generate permanent gem bonuses (discounts), accumulating prestige points along the way. The first player to reach **15 prestige points** triggers the endgame; after all players complete that round, the winner is determined.

**Core loop:** Collect gems → Buy cards → Earn discounts → Buy better cards → Attract nobles → Reach 15 points.

---

## 2. Components

### Gems (Tokens)
| Colour | Type | Count (4P / 3P / 2P) |
|--------|------|----------------------|
| Red | Ruby | 7 / 5 / 4 |
| Green | Emerald | 7 / 5 / 4 |
| Blue | Sapphire | 7 / 5 / 4 |
| White | Diamond | 7 / 5 / 4 |
| Black | Onyx | 7 / 5 / 4 |
| Yellow | Gold (wild) | 5 (always) |

Gold gems are wildcards — they substitute for any colour when buying a card.

### Development Cards (90 total)
- Organised into **3 levels** (Level 1: cheap/low points, Level 3: expensive/high points).
- Each card shows: **gem cost** (what you pay), **bonus gem colour** (permanent discount), **prestige points** (0–5).
- At game start, 4 cards per level are laid face-up. The rest form face-down decks.

### Noble Tiles (10 total)
- Each noble requires a specific combination of **bonus gems** (purchased card colours) to visit.
- Each noble grants **3 prestige points**.
- Number of nobles in play = number of players + 1.

---

## 3. Setup

1. Place all gem tokens in the centre (bank), using the correct counts for your player count.
2. Shuffle each level deck separately and place them in rows. Reveal 4 cards face-up per level.
3. Randomly select (player count + 1) noble tiles and place them visible to all.
4. Randomly determine the first player.

---

## 4. Turn Structure

On your turn, choose **exactly one** of the following four actions:

---

### Action 1 — Take 3 Gems of Different Colours
- Pick up to 3 gem tokens, each of a **different colour** (no gold).
- You may take fewer than 3 if fewer than 3 colours are available.
- Each colour taken must have at least 1 gem in the bank.

**Example:** Take 1 Ruby + 1 Emerald + 1 Sapphire.

---

### Action 2 — Take 2 Gems of the Same Colour
- Take **2 gems of the same colour**.
- Only allowed if the bank has **at least 4** of that colour.

**Example:** Take 2 Diamonds (bank must have ≥ 4 Diamonds).

---

### Action 3 — Reserve a Card
- Take any **1 face-up card** from the board, OR the **top face-down card** from any level deck (without looking).
- Place the reserved card in your hand (visible to others). Maximum **3 reserved cards** at a time.
- Take **1 Gold gem** from the bank (if any are available).
- This is the only way to acquire gold gems.

**Strategic use:** Reserve a card your opponent needs, or secure a high-value card you can't yet afford.

---

### Action 4 — Buy a Development Card
- Purchase a **face-up board card** or one of your own **reserved cards**.
- **Cost calculation:** Pay the card's gem cost, minus your **bonus gems** (one bonus gem = one free gem of that colour).
- **Gold as wildcard:** Each gold gem you spend covers 1 gem of any colour you're short on.
- Gems paid go back to the bank.
- The purchased card's **bonus gem** is now permanently yours (counts as a free gem for future purchases).

**Example:** Card costs 2 Ruby, 3 Emerald. You have 1 Ruby bonus + 2 Emerald bonus. Effective cost: 1 Ruby, 1 Emerald. Pay those from your tokens; keep the card.

---

### After Your Action — Discard Excess Gems
- If you now hold **more than 10 gems**, discard gems back to the bank until you have exactly 10.

---

### After Your Action — Noble Visits (Automatic)
- Check every remaining noble tile. If your **total bonus gems** (purchased card colours) meet a noble's requirements, that noble visits you.
- You take the noble tile (3 prestige points). No action required.
- If you qualify for multiple nobles simultaneously, **you choose one**.
- Each noble can only visit once and is removed from the game after visiting.

---

## 5. Endgame & Winning

### Trigger
- When any player reaches **15 or more prestige points** at the end of their turn, the endgame is triggered.
- **All other players complete their turns** so everyone has had the same number of turns.

### Determining the Winner
1. Player with the **most prestige points** wins.
2. **Tie-breaker:** If two or more players are tied on prestige points, the player with the **fewest purchased development cards** wins.
3. If still tied after the tie-breaker, those players share the victory.

---

## 6. Prestige Points Summary

| Source | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Development card | 0–5 (depends on card) |
| Noble tile | 3 (fixed) |

---

## 7. Key Strategic Concepts

- **Engine building:** Early low-level cards create discounts that make later expensive cards affordable.
- **Tempo:** Taking gems efficiently each turn is critical; wasted turns fall behind.
- **Blocking:** Reserve a card an opponent needs (at cost of a turn and limited hand space).
- **Gold management:** Gold is scarce (only 5 tokens). Reserve wisely; gold lets you bridge shortfalls.
- **Nobles planning:** Knowing which nobles are in play lets you target the bonus colours that attract them.

## 8. Step-by-Step Game Walkthrough (2-Player Example)

This walkthrough illustrates a typical 2-player game between **Alice** (human) and **Bob** (AI). The winning target is 15 prestige points.

### Setup
- Gem bank: 4 of each colour + 5 gold
- 4 face-up cards per level (12 visible total)
- 3 noble tiles in play

---

### Round 1

**Alice's Turn 1**
- Sees a promising Level 1 card costing 1 Ruby, 1 Emerald, 1 Sapphire.
- **Action:** Take 3 different gems — Ruby, Emerald, Sapphire.
- Gems held: 1R, 1G (green/Emerald), 1S (Sapphire). Total: 3.

**Bob's Turn 1**
- Spots a Level 1 card costing 2 Emerald, 1 Diamond.
- **Action:** Take 3 different gems — Emerald, Emerald... wait, can't take 2 same unless ≥ 4 in bank.
- Bank has 4 Emerald. **Action:** Take 2 Emeralds (same colour, bank had 4).
- Gems held: 2 Emerald. Total: 2.

---

### Round 2

**Alice's Turn 2**
- Wants to save up for a Level 2 card. Needs more gems.
- **Action:** Take 3 different gems — Diamond, Onyx, Ruby.
- Gems held: 2R, 1E, 1S, 1D, 1O. Total: 6.

**Bob's Turn 2**
- Needs 1 more Diamond to buy a Level 1 card (costs 2E + 1D).
- **Action:** Take 3 different gems — Diamond, Ruby, Sapphire.
- Gems held: 2E, 1D, 1R, 1S. Total: 5.

---

### Round 3

**Alice's Turn 3**
- Spots a great Level 2 card worth 3 prestige points but can't afford it yet. Wants to secure it.
- **Action:** Reserve the Level 2 card (face-up). Takes 1 Gold from bank.
- Gems held: 2R, 1E, 1S, 1D, 1O, 1 Gold. Total: 7. Reserved cards: 1.

**Bob's Turn 3**
- Has 2E + 1D — enough to buy the Level 1 card (costs 2E + 1D).
- **Action:** Buy Level 1 card. Pays 2E + 1D back to bank. Gains: 1 Sapphire bonus, 0 prestige points.
- Gems held: 1R, 1S. Bonus gems: 1 Sapphire. Cards purchased: 1.
- Noble check: no noble requirements met yet.

---

### Round 4

**Alice's Turn 4**
- Has 2R, 1E, 1S, 1D, 1O, 1 Gold. Reserved card costs 2R, 3E, 1S.
- Effective cost (no bonuses yet): 2R + 3E + 1S = 6 gems. Has only 1E.
- Short 2E — can use Gold to cover 1E, still short 1E. Can't buy yet.
- **Action:** Take 3 different gems — Emerald, Emerald... bank has 3E (started 4, Bob took 2, Alice took 1). Can't take 2 same (need ≥ 4).
- **Action:** Take 3 different gems — Emerald, Diamond, Sapphire.
- Gems held: 2R, 2E, 2S, 2D, 1O, 1 Gold. Total: 10. (At limit!)

**Bob's Turn 4**
- Wants to buy another Level 1 card (costs 1R, 1E, 1S, 1D). Has 1R, 1S + 1S bonus.
- Effective cost: 1R + 1E + 0S + 1D. Has 1R, 1S tokens but needs 1E and 1D.
- Not affordable yet.
- **Action:** Take 3 different gems — Emerald, Diamond, Onyx.
- Gems held: 1R, 1S, 1E, 1D, 1O. Total: 5. Bonus: 1S.

---

### Round 5

**Alice's Turn 5**
- Reserved card costs 2R, 3E, 1S. Has 2R, 2E, 2S, 2D, 1O, 1 Gold.
- Short 1E — use Gold! Effective payment: 2R + 2E + 1S + 1 Gold.
- **Action:** Buy reserved card. Pays 2R, 2E, 1S, 1 Gold to bank. Gains: Emerald bonus, 3 prestige points.
- Gems held: 0R, 0E, 1S, 2D, 1O. Total: 4. Bonus gems: 1 Emerald. Points: 3.
- Noble check: Noble A requires 3E + 3S bonus — not yet.

**Bob's Turn 5**
- Now has 1R, 1S, 1E, 1D, 1O + 1S bonus. Card costs 1R, 1E, 1S, 1D. Effective: 1R + 1E + 0S + 1D.
- Has exactly 1R, 1E, 1D!
- **Action:** Buy Level 1 card. Pays 1R, 1E, 1D. Gains: Ruby bonus, 0 prestige points.
- Gems held: 1S, 1O. Bonus: 1S + 1R. Cards: 2.

---

### Rounds 6–10 (Summary)

- **Alice** continues engine-building: buys 2 more Level 1 cards (Sapphire bonus, Ruby bonus), then targets Level 2 and Level 3 cards.
- **Bob** builds up Ruby and Emerald bonuses, making Level 2 cards effectively cheap.

---

### Endgame Trigger (Round 11)

**Alice's Turn 11**
- Prestige points so far: 3 (Level 2 card) + 2 (Level 2 card) + 4 (Level 3 card) + 3 (Noble visited) = 12.
- Buys a Level 3 card worth 4 prestige points. Points: **16**.
- Noble check: Noble B (requires 4R + 4E bonus) — Alice has 4R + 4E bonus! Noble visits.
- Points: 16 + 3 = **19**. Endgame triggered.

**Bob must complete his Turn 11.**

**Bob's Turn 11**
- Points: 11. Buys a Level 3 card worth 4 points → 15 points.
- Noble check: Noble C visits Bob too (he also qualifies). Bob: 15 + 3 = **18** points.

---

### Result

| Player | Prestige Points | Development Cards |
|--------|----------------|------------------|
| Alice  | 19 | 8 |
| Bob | 18 | 7 |

Alice wins with 19 points (most prestige points).

> **Tie-breaker example:** If both had 18 points, Bob would win — he has 7 cards vs Alice's 8 (fewer cards wins).

---

## 9. Common Beginner Mistakes

1. **Forgetting to check nobles** after buying a card — nobles visit automatically but must be checked.
2. **Exceeding 10 gems** — must discard before the next turn.
3. **Trying to take 2 same gems** when the bank has fewer than 4 — illegal move.
4. **Over-reserving** — holding 3 reserved cards blocks future reservations; only reserve when strategic.
5. **Ignoring endgame timing** — everyone gets equal turns after the trigger, so being second player gives a chance to catch up.
